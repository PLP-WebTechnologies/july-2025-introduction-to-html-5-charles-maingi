Introduction

This page demonstrates how to use HTML5 semantic elements effectively while incorporating accessibility and SEO best practices.

Web Accessibility

Accessibility ensures that all users, including those with disabilities, can navigate and interact with websites. Use semantic tags and ARIA labels to help screen readers understand your content structure.

Why It Matters

Accessible websites are not only inclusive but also meet legal standards and improve user experience for everyone.

SEO Basics

Search Engine Optimization (SEO) improves your site's visibility on search engines. Use descriptive content, proper heading hierarchy, and meaningful metadata.

Key Tips for SEO

  • Use keywords naturally in content.
  • Structure headings from <h1> to <h6>.
  • Provide a concise <meta description> in the head.

Conclusion

By focusing on semantic HTML5, accessibility, and SEO, you lay the foundation for building websites that are usable, discoverable, and future-friendly.
